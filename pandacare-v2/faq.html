Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about getting medical treatment in China with PandaCare

Getting Started
How does PandaCare work?

It's simple: you tell us about your medical needs, and we handle everything else. Our process:

  • Consultation — Share your condition and medical records with us (free)
  • Matching — We recommend the best hospital and specialist for your case
  • Planning — We arrange appointments, visa support, travel, and accommodation
  • Treatment — Your English-speaking companion accompanies you to every appointment
  • Follow-up — We provide translated medical reports and remote follow-up support
How long does the entire process take?

From first contact to your appointment in China, it typically takes 7-14 days. For urgent cases, we can fast-track to as few as 3-5 days. The treatment timeline depends on your specific needs — a health check may be done in one day, while surgery may require 1-3 weeks including recovery.

What medical conditions can you help with?

We cover a wide range of specialties including:

  • Dental (implants, veneers, orthodontics)
  • Ophthalmology (LASIK, cataract surgery)
  • Orthopedics (joint replacement, spine surgery)
  • Cardiology
  • Oncology (second opinions, treatment plans)
  • Traditional Chinese Medicine & rehabilitation
  • Comprehensive health screening
  • Cosmetic/plastic surgery

If your condition isn't listed, contact us — we can likely help.

Cost & Payment
How much does treatment in China cost compared to my country?

On average, you can save 50-80% compared to the US, UK, Canada, or Australia. Some examples:

  • Dental implant: $600–$1,200 (vs. $3,000–$5,000 in the US)
  • Full health screening: $300–$800 (vs. $2,000+ in the US)
  • MRI scan: $80–$150 (vs. $1,000–$3,000 in the US)
  • Hip replacement: $8,000–$12,000 (vs. $40,000+ in the US)
  • LASIK: $1,200–$2,000 (vs. $4,000–$6,000 in the US)

These prices are at top-tier, JCI-accredited hospitals — not budget clinics.

What does PandaCare's service fee include?

Our service fee covers:

  • Pre-trip consultation and treatment planning
  • Hospital and doctor matching
  • Appointment scheduling
  • Dedicated English-speaking medical companion
  • Real-time translation during all appointments
  • Local transportation coordination
  • Post-treatment report translation
  • Follow-up communication with your doctor

Medical fees, flights, and accommodation are billed separately and transparently.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept international credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, Amex), PayPal, and bank wire transfer. Hospital fees can typically be paid by card directly at the hospital. We provide a transparent cost breakdown before you commit.

Travel & Visa
Do I need a visa to travel to China for medical treatment?

Most nationalities need a visa. However, China currently offers:

  • 144-hour visa-free transit in many cities (enough for short treatments)
  • 15-day visa-free for citizens of many countries
  • Medical visa (M visa) for longer treatments

We provide an official hospital invitation letter and assist with your visa application. Many of our patients use the visa-free transit policy for routine treatments.

Do you help with flights and accommodation?

Yes! We offer:

  • Flight booking assistance and route recommendations
  • Airport pickup and drop-off service
  • Hotel recommendations near your hospital (budget to luxury)
  • Extended-stay apartment options for longer treatments

We can also arrange sightseeing and cultural experiences during your recovery days.

Which cities in China do you operate in?

We partner with top hospitals in major cities including:

  • Beijing — Peking Union, Beijing Friendship Hospital
  • Shanghai — Huashan Hospital, Ruijin Hospital
  • Guangzhou — Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hospital
  • Shenzhen — HKU-Shenzhen Hospital
  • Chengdu, Hangzhou, Xi'an — and more

We match you with the best hospital for your specific condition, regardless of city.

Safety & Quality
How do I know the hospitals are safe and qualified?

We only partner with:

  • Class-A Tertiary Hospitals — China's highest tier (equivalent to major academic medical centers)
  • JCI-accredited facilities — the international gold standard for healthcare quality
  • Hospitals with dedicated international patient departments

Many of our partner hospitals have doctors trained at Harvard, Johns Hopkins, Mayo Clinic, and other top institutions. We verify credentials for every doctor we recommend.

What if something goes wrong during treatment?

Your safety is our top priority:

  • Your companion is with you at every medical interaction
  • We have 24/7 emergency support in English
  • All partner hospitals have international patient insurance acceptance
  • We recommend travel medical insurance and can help you find the right policy
  • Post-treatment complications are handled by the same medical team with our ongoing translation support
Will my doctor back home accept the medical records from China?

Yes. We provide fully translated medical records in English, including imaging results, lab work, surgical reports, and medication lists. These follow international medical documentation standards and are readily accepted by healthcare providers worldwide. We can also arrange direct communication between your Chinese doctor and your home physician if needed.

Language & Communication
I don't speak Chinese. Will that be a problem?

Not at all — that's exactly what we're here for. Your dedicated medical companion:

  • Speaks fluent English (and often other languages)
  • Has medical terminology training
  • Accompanies you to every appointment, test, and procedure
  • Translates in real-time between you and your medical team
  • Helps you navigate daily life (ordering food, transportation, etc.)

Many of our partner hospitals also have English-speaking staff in their international departments.
